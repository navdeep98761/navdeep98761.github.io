Logical Operators

In JavaScript, there are four fundamental logical operators that allow developers to manipulate and control the flow of their programs: && (logical AND), || (logical OR), ! (logical NOT), and ?? (nullish coalescing). The logical AND (&&) operator evaluates to true if both of its operands are true. The logical OR (||) operator evaluates to true if at least one of its operands is true. The logical NOT (!) operator negates the truthiness of an expression, turning true into false and vice versa. The nullish coalescing (??) operator is used to provide a default value when dealing with potentially null or undefined variables.
